Mbesses

Description

Mbesses, Mtabka or Mtekba this delicious Algerian galette that melt in your mouth delicious. Made of semolina and butter , very similar to Bradj or Makrout or even the Moroccan harcha. This galette is very easy and quick to make.In Kabylie we have the cake with olive oil. This is often served with mint tea or coffee latte for breakfast or to enjoyed with an afternoon cuppa. I like to generously drizzle with honey that's perfumed with orange blossom water, but you can optionally dip the galettes in the honey. These galettes are also traditionally served us with buttermilk for suhoor (the meal before the fast) during the holy month of Ramadan.

Ingredients

  • 400 gr of durum wheat semolina flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1Â½ cups of tepid milk
  • 1 packet of yeast
  • 2 cups of sugar
  • 1 cup of honey
  • 150 g of almonds as an optional garnish
  • water
  • 2 tablespoons of butter

Steps

  1. Knead the flour, eggs, sugar, milk and yeast together with some water until the ingredients are incorporated.
  2. The dough should be wetter and looser than a bread dough.
  3. Heat a skillet for just a minute and add the butter.
  4. Remove pan from heat.
  5. Press the dough down into the skillet.
  6. Cover the dough with plastic or a slightly damp towel and let it rise at room temperature until approximately double in size.
  7. Cook on very, very low heat.
  8. The cake will rise to about double in volume.
  9. When fully cooked it will become firm.
  10. Turn over and brown the other side for about 10 minutes.
  11. Brush the top with warmed honey this will obviously moisten the cake.
  12. You can add orange flower water or rose water to the honey if you like.
  13. Garnishing with almonds is optional.
  14. You can also toast the almonds if you like.
